# ECONOMICS.md — Unit Economics Model (BURNRATE AI)

> All numbers below are directional operating assumptions for evaluating whether BURNRATE AI could become a viable lead-generation and AI spend optimization product for Credex. They are not claims of current revenue or customer traction.

---

## Core assumption

BURNRATE AI is modeled primarily as a:

> low-friction lead-generation funnel for AI-native startups with growing tooling spend.

The product’s value is not only the audit itself, but the downstream opportunity to convert high-intent operators into:

* Credex consultations
* spend optimization engagements
* recurring AI infrastructure advisory relationships

---

## What a converted lead is worth

Assume the typical target company:

* has 15–60 employees
* spends \$2k–\$15k/month on AI tooling + APIs
* lacks centralized AI cost governance

If Credex helps reduce AI spend by even 15–25%, the financial impact becomes meaningful quickly.

### Example company

* AI tooling spend: \$6,000/month
* Potential optimization identified: 20%
* Annual savings opportunity: \$14,400/year

If Credex monetized this through:

* advisory retainers
* optimization consulting
* infrastructure partnerships
* recurring platform subscriptions

a reasonable estimated contract value could be:

| Engagement type | Estimated value |
|----------------|----------------|
| One-time optimization engagement | \$2k–\$5k |
| Ongoing advisory / SaaS subscription | \$3k–\$12k ARR |

For modeling purposes:

> assume one converted Credex customer is worth roughly \$5,000 ARR on average.

---

## CAC assumptions by channel

The GTM strategy intentionally prioritizes low-cost founder distribution channels.

### 1. Founder teardown posts

Channels:

* X/Twitter
* Indie Hackers
* Reddit
* founder Slack groups

Estimated CAC:

| Metric | Estimate |
|--------|----------|
| Content views per teardown | 2,000 |
| Audit conversions | 20 |
| Consultation calls | 2 |
| Closed customer rate | 0.3–0.5 |

Because distribution is organic:

> effective CAC could remain under \$100/customer initially.

---

### 2. Direct outbound to AI startups

Target companies:

* actively hiring AI engineers
* visibly AI-native
* likely scaling API usage quickly

Estimated economics:

| Metric | Estimate |
|--------|----------|
| 100 outbound messages | 8–12 audit completions |
| Consultation conversion | 20–30% |
| Customer conversion | 10–15% of calls |

Main cost here is founder/operator time rather than ad spend.

Estimated CAC:

> \$150–\$400/customer depending on outreach efficiency.

---

### 3. Share-link virality

Every audit creates a shareable result URL.

If operators forward reports internally:

* engineering → finance
* CTO → founder
* founder → operations

then one audit can generate multiple qualified viewers.

This creates a compounding acquisition effect with near-zero marginal distribution cost.

Projected CAC:

> effectively \$0–\$50 for referral-driven usage.

---

## Conversion math required for profitability

Assume:

| Funnel stage | Conversion |
|--------------|------------|
| Landing page → audit completion | 30% |
| Audit completion → consultation booked | 10% |
| Consultation → paying customer | 20% |

Example:

10,000 landing visitors/month

↓

3,000 completed audits

↓

300 consultation calls

↓

60 paying customers

If average customer value is:

> \$5,000 ARR

Then:

60 × \$5,000 = \$300,000 ARR added from one strong acquisition cycle.

Because infrastructure costs are relatively low:

* Vercel
* Supabase
* deterministic computation
* no expensive inference layer

gross margins remain high unless heavy human consulting is added.

---

## What must be true to reach \$1M ARR in 18 months

To reach \$1M ARR:

| Metric | Requirement |
|--------|-------------|
| Average customer value | \$5k ARR |
| Required customers | ~200 |
| Monthly new customers needed | ~11–12/month |

That becomes realistic only if:

### 1. Audit distribution compounds organically

The share-link mechanic must create internal forwarding loops inside startups.

### 2. AI tooling sprawl continues growing

The market pain must intensify as startups adopt:

* multiple copilots
* multiple APIs
* overlapping subscriptions

### 3. Credex becomes associated with AI cost optimization

The positioning must evolve from:

> “interesting calculator”

to:

> “trusted AI infrastructure optimization platform.”

### 4. Deterministic trust becomes a differentiator

Many AI products generate opaque recommendations.

BURNRATE AI instead emphasizes:

* explainability
* reproducibility
* pricing traceability
* deterministic outputs

That trust layer could become a meaningful competitive advantage for finance-aware buyers.

---

## Key economic insight

The business works only if:

> audit outputs are trusted enough to trigger operational conversations.

The audit itself is not the final product.

The real value comes from becoming:

* the system teams use to understand AI tooling spend
* the trusted optimization layer between engineering and finance
* the entry point into higher-value Credex relationships
